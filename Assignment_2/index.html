Accidents in United Kingdom (2010 - 2014)

Car accidents occur when there is a collision involving vehicles, pedestrians, animals or any other objects such as tree, traffic lights. There are different factors which leads to different accident severity such as weather of the day or brand of the car.

Did you know? Despite having large population in Scotland, the accident rate is the lowest among the all region. In contrast, Yorkshire and The Humber which have the similar population as Scotland has the second highest accident rate.

Does different weather and car brand affect the number of accidents?

Accidents happen the most on fine day while accident happen the least when there is mists or fogs. The distribution of accidents across different weathers are expected as fine weather occurs most frequently while mist or fog rarely happen. Different car brands have different durability, this could also be a factor in number of accidents and its severity.

Throughout the year, the number of accidents happened is the lowest during the end of December and early January because most public holiday falls in that period. Since most businesses are closed, there are lesser traffic on the road.
